Who I Am

About Me

I am an aerospace engineer with hands-on experimental and computational expertise, and a freshly completed Master's thesis on Effects of Surface Wettability on Cavitation Inception & Cloud Cavitation at Virginia Tech.

My work sits at the intersection of fluid dynamics, materials science, and data-driven analysis using tools like PIV, SEM, FTIR, XRD, and machine learning to uncover how physical surfaces govern complex flow behavior.

Beyond the laboratory environment, I'm intrigued by big questions on planetary habitability, astrobiology, and how engineering methods can study life in extreme environments.
